About

From Tech Growth to Energy Transition

I'm an economist who spent a decade scaling technology companies. Most recently, I led Strategy & Finance at Google Fiber, where I managed $1bn+ in revenue planning and helped drive the business to EBITDA profitability—growing from 400k to 1M+ subscribers.

Now I'm shifting my focus to energy systems. Why? Because the skills translate perfectly, and the mission matters more.

Why Energy?

01

Similar Business Models

Both utilities and ISPs require heavy upfront investment (fiber networks or power plants), then monetize through monthly subscriptions. The economic fundamentals are nearly identical.

02

Forecasting is Everything

At Google Fiber, I led planning and forecasting. In energy, forecasting is the business— balancing supply and demand to avoid imbalance penalties. The technical skills transfer directly.

03

Mission-Driven Work

I started my career in ESG investments at KfW (German Development Bank). The Energiewende combines everything I care about: reducing dependence on fossil fuel autocracies, protecting vulnerable communities from pollution, and mitigating climate change.

Background

  • Google (2020-2025) - Strategy & Finance Manager, Google Fiber. Managed $1bn+ P&L, scaled subscriber base 2.5x, led ERP migration (Oracle → SAP).
  • Orda Technologies (2016-2019) - CFO. Built financial architecture for VC fundraising, led to successful acquisition.
  • UBS (2015-2018) - Private Equity Product Specialist. Executed 10+ capital raises for pre-IPO tech firms including Grab.
  • KfW (2012-2013) - Investment Analyst. Managed $80M in infrastructure proposals across energy and telecom.

Education
