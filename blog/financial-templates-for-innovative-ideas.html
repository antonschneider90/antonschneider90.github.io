Financial Templates for Innovative Ideas

Seeing Hundreds of Business Plans — From the Other Side of the Table

Early in my career, I worked in private equity at UBS. My job was to analyze hundreds of pre-IPO tech business plans and identify the most attractive ones. I loved it. I got to see innovative companies from all sorts of industries — health care, data analytics, social media, electric mobility — and learn what made their financials tick.

Over time, I realized that I wanted to be on the other side. Not evaluating business plans, but building them. Working directly inside companies, helping them find investors, scale their product, and grow their customer base.

Employee #1

So I partnered up with friends in Munich who were founding a food ordering business, and I joined as employee #1. Having seen so many business plans during my time in private equity, I had a solid sense of what it takes to raise money. How to structure a financial model. How to run scenarios that surface the real risks and opportunities. What strategy and KPIs to present to investors.

What I didn't have was templates. I couldn't take models from other companies and repurpose them — I had to build everything from scratch.

Sharing the Playbook

That experience is why I now like to share my work. Too many founders have great ideas but lack the financial background to turn them into a compelling model. They know their product inside out, but struggle to translate that into a P&L, a cash flow forecast, or a set of investor-ready scenarios.

I've created a SaaS financial template with an interactive dashboard, available on this site. It lets you adjust key metrics — subscription price, churn, customer acquisition cost — and instantly see how they affect your financial statements and investor returns. I hope it helps founders play with their numbers, understand their business model better, and get a clearer picture of how to pitch it. This model is for monthly subscription business. Please reach out if you have other interesting use cases you'd like to discuss.

Why Europe Needs More of This

I believe in the power of entrepreneurship: finding new solutions to problems and unlocking new economic sectors. And I see tremendous potential in Europe specifically.

The European startup ecosystem has matured significantly. The continent now counts over 400 unicorns. Venture capital investment reached roughly €66 billion in 2025, and the region's share of global VC has grown from 5% two decades ago to about 20% today. In 2025, Germany overtook the UK in VC deal value for the first time, and French and German firms dominated the top European fundraising rankings.

But the gap with the US remains wide. American tech companies attracted $177 billion in private investment in just the first nine months of 2025 — almost double the prior year. US tech funding runs at roughly 0.6–0.7% of GDP, while the UK sits at 0.35% and most of continental Europe well below that. European VC fundraising hit its lowest level in a decade in 2025, and institutional investors still allocate far less to venture capital than their US counterparts. The structural funding gap for rounds above $15 million is estimated at around $75 billion.

There are reasons for optimism. US investors are increasingly participating in European deals, drawn by lower valuations and strong technical talent. The EU adopted a Startup and Scaleup Strategy in late 2025 to put entrepreneurship at the centre of its competitiveness agenda. Energy, defence, and AI are attracting record investment across the continent.

Europe doesn't lack ideas or talent. What it often lacks is the financial infrastructure that make it easier for founders to go from concept to funded company. Templates and tools are a small piece of that gap that I'd like to help close.
